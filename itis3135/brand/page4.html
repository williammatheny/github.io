Common household poisions and toxins for animals

In our homes there a several food products, substances, items, etc that may be safe for humans but extremely deadly towards our pets. A lot of these items are ones we probably don't think of at all, but it is very important to be aware of these.

  • Avocados / Foods with large pits - Certain foods like avacdos and peaches can be really harmful towards a pets digestive system. The pits are really big and a pet might not know of the dangers of that and will swallow it anyway. This is really dangerous.
  • Bread Dough - When ingested the unbaked dough will expand, ferment, and release carbon dioxide and alcohol into the bloodstream. This can cause a bloated stomach and will be very dangerous for a pet. It can lead to several problems and is important to do something if you notice your pet got a hold of some.
  • Chocolate - Dogs and many other pets are very allergic to chocolate. 95% of emergency calls regarding pets are due to accidental chocolate consumption from dogs. This can lead to the dog having methylxznthine poisoning, diarrhea, vomiting, hyperactivity, seizures and abormal heart rhythm, and even death.
  • Chicken bones - Leaving chicken bones or other similar bones on the top of a trash can or a table that a dog can reach is very dangerous. They will not know the dangers of eating a bone like that and it can easily disrupt the wind pipe of that animal and cause it to choke.

How to promote Pet Safety in your home

  • Using a Pet Sticker - Putting a pet sticker on your home in case of emergencies like fire are very important. The main cause for pets dying in house fires are because they are missed by the fire fighters who didn't even know you had a pet. This lets them know that they are there and can be saved. Pet Stickers can be provided from a veternarian if you don't have any.
  • "Pet Proofing" your home - Just like a child where you "baby proof" your home for their safety, this is also really important for your pets. Make sure to not leave dangerous substances out where the pet can reach, or open flames that can animal can knock over. Train your pet to sleep in one specfic are and it will become their safe space. They will know to go here if anything bad happens at the house.
  • Understanding your Pet - It is very important to understand our pets and their personalities. It is important to know what your pet will do in certain situations and how they'll react. This can definitely help you a lot and will make it a lot safer for both you and them in many situations.
